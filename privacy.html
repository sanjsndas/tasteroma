Privacy Policy for Tasteroma

At Tasteroma, accessible from Tasteroma.com, one of our main priorities is the privacy of our visitors. This Privacy Policy document contains types of information that is collected and recorded by Tasteroma and how we use it. We are committed to protecting your personal data and respecting your privacy rights. This policy outlines our practices regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of your information when you use our service, and tells you about your privacy rights and how the law protects you.

If you have additional questions or require more information about our Privacy Policy, do not hesitate to contact us. Your trust is paramount to us, and we strive for full transparency in our data practices.

Consent

By accessing and using our website, Tasteroma.com, you explicitly consent to the terms of this Privacy Policy. This includes the collection, processing, and storage of your information as described herein. If you do not agree with any part of this policy, please refrain from using our services. Your continued use of the website after any modifications to this policy will constitute your acknowledgment of the modifications and your consent to abide and be bound by the modified Privacy Policy.

Information We Collect

We collect various types of information to provide and improve our service to you. The specific personal information requested, and the reasons for its collection, will always be made clear to you at the point of collection. We aim to collect only the data that is necessary for the stated purposes.

Information you voluntarily provide: If you choose to contact us directly, for instance, by sending an email, filling out a contact form, or participating in surveys, we may receive and store additional information about you. This can include your name, email address, phone number, the content of your message, any attachments you send, and other information you voluntarily provide. This data is used to respond to your inquiries, provide customer support, and improve our services based on your feedback.

Account Registration Data: When you register for a Tasteroma account, we will ask for necessary contact information. This typically includes your name, email address, and potentially a username and password. We may also request demographic information or preferences related to your culinary interests to personalize your experience, such as your preferred cuisines, dietary restrictions, or favorite spices. This information is crucial for creating and managing your personalized profile, allowing you to save recipes, track your cooking progress, and interact with the Tasteroma community.

User-Generated Content: If you participate in our "Community Creations" or other interactive features, you may submit recipes, photos, comments, and cooking tips. This content, along with any associated metadata (e.g., date and time of submission), will be collected. Please be mindful of the information you share publicly, as it may be visible to other users.

How We Use Your Information

The information we collect from you is utilized for a variety of purposes, all aimed at enhancing your experience on Tasteroma and ensuring the effective operation of our platform:

  • To Provide, Operate, and Maintain Our Website: This includes ensuring the website functions correctly, providing access to recipes and features, and maintaining system stability.
  • To Improve, Personalize, and Expand Our Website: We analyze user behavior and preferences to develop new features, refine existing ones, and tailor the content to better suit your interests, such as recommending recipes based on your viewing history.
  • To Understand and Analyze How You Use Our Website: We use analytics tools to monitor usage patterns, identify popular content, and understand areas for improvement. This helps us make informed decisions about content development and user interface design.
  • To Develop New Products, Services, Features, and Functionality: Your feedback and usage data can inspire new offerings, whether it's a new recipe category, an interactive tool, or a premium service.
  • To Communicate with You: We may communicate with you directly or through our trusted partners for various reasons, including:
    • Customer Service: Responding to your inquiries, providing technical support, and addressing any issues you may encounter.
    • Updates and Information: Notifying you about changes to our terms, new features, important announcements, or security alerts.
    • Marketing and Promotional Purposes: Sending you newsletters, special offers, and information about new recipes, spice kits, or events that we believe might be of interest to you, always in accordance with your communication preferences.
  • To Send You Emails: Beyond transactional emails related to your account or service, we may send promotional emails if you have opted in to receive them. You can manage your email preferences at any time.
  • To Find and Prevent Fraud: We use collected information to monitor for and prevent fraudulent activities, unauthorized access, and other malicious uses of our platform, ensuring a secure environment for all users.
  • For Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, legal processes, or governmental requests.

Log Files

Tasteroma adheres to a standard procedure of utilizing log files. These files automatically record information about visitors when they access websites. This is a common practice across almost all hosting companies and constitutes a part of hosting services' analytics. The data collected by log files typically includes internet protocol (IP) addresses, the type of browser you are using, your Internet Service Provider (ISP), date and time stamps of your visits, referring and exit pages, and potentially the number of clicks you make on various elements. It's important to note that this information is not linked to any data that is personally identifiable. The primary purpose of this collected information is for analyzing trends in website usage, administering the site effectively, tracking users' movement patterns within the website to understand popular sections, and gathering broad demographic information to better serve our user base. This helps us optimize site performance and content delivery without identifying individual users.

Cookies and Web Beacons

Similar to most modern websites, Tasteroma employs 'cookies' to enhance your browsing experience. Cookies are small data files placed on your device. These cookies are primarily used to store information, including visitors' preferences and records of the pages on the website that the visitor has accessed or visited. The information gathered from cookies is instrumental in optimizing the users' experience by customizing our web page content. For example, we might tailor content based on your browser type, language preferences, or other information to make your visits more efficient and personalized. This allows us to remember your settings, log you in automatically, or suggest content relevant to your interests. By continuing to use our site, you agree to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

Google, as one of our third-party vendors, utilizes cookies, specifically known as DART cookies, to serve advertisements to our site visitors. These DART cookies enable Google to serve ads to our users based on their visit to Tasteroma.com and other websites across the internet. This allows for more relevant advertising content. CCPA Privacy Rights (Do Not Sell My Personal Information)

For residents of California, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) grants specific rights regarding your personal data. Among these rights, California consumers have the right to:

  • Request Disclosure: You have the right to request that a business that collects your personal data disclose the categories and specific pieces of personal data that the business has collected about you.
  • Request Deletion: You have the right to request that a business delete any personal data about you that the business has collected, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Opt-Out of Sale: You have the right to request that a business that sells your personal data, not sell your personal data.

Upon receiving a verifiable request, we are legally obligated to respond to you within one month. If you wish to exercise any of these rights under the CCPA, please do not hesitate to contact us using the contact information provided at the bottom of this policy. We are committed to facilitating your rights as a California consumer.

GDPR Data Protection Rights

For users residing in the European Economic Area (EEA) and the UK, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) provides you with comprehensive data protection rights. We want to ensure you are fully aware of all your entitlements:

  • The right to access: You have the right to request copies of your personal data that we hold. Please note that we may charge a small administrative fee for this service to cover the costs associated with providing the information.
  • The right to rectification: You have the right to request that we correct any information you believe is inaccurate or incomplete. We are committed to maintaining accurate data and will update our records promptly upon your request.
  • The right to erasure ("right to be forgotten"): You have the right to request that we erase your personal data under certain conditions, such as when the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected or if you withdraw consent.
  • The right to restrict processing: You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data under certain conditions, for example, if you contest the accuracy of the data or the lawfulness of the processing.
  • The right to object to processing: You have the right to object to our processing of your personal data under certain conditions, particularly if the processing is based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • The right to data portability: You have the right to request that we transfer the data that we have collected to another organization, or directly to you, in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, under certain conditions.

We commit to responding to all legitimate requests concerning your GDPR rights within one month of receipt. If you would like to exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided at the end of this Privacy Policy. We take these rights seriously and are dedicated to upholding them.
